The life of Benjamin Franklin
1706-1790


1706 Benjamin Franklin was born on january 17 in Boston massachusetts

1718-1723 From the years Benjamin Franklin worked for his brother, James Franklin as an apprentice

1723 he moved to Philadelphia Pennsylvania

1727 Benjamin Founded the Junto, a debating club in Philadelphia

1729 Purchased the Pennsylvania Gazette

1731 Established the Library Company of Philadelphia

1732 Published Poor Richard's Almanak

1737 appointed postmaster of Philadelphia

1743 proposed formation of the american philosophical society

1748 retired from the printing business

1751 he published Experiments and observations on electricity and elected into pennsylvania assembly

1753 Appointed joint deputy postmaster general of North America

1754 Represented Pennsylvania at the Albany Congress

1764-1775 In London, England, as agent for Pennsylvania and other colonies

1771 he started his autobiography

1775 Elected a delegate to the Second Continental Congress

1776 Helped draft the decloration of independence and signed treaties witth france

1783 signed the tready of paris

1785 Elected president, Supreme Executive Council of Pennsylvania

1787 Represented Pennsylvania at the Constitutional Convention

1790 Signed antislavery petition to Congress as president of the Pennsylvania Society for Promoting the Abolition of Slavery

1790 Died in Philadelphia and interred in the city's Christ Church Burial Ground
